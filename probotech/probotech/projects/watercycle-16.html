water cycle

what is it?

The water cycle, is a fundamental Earth process, and a dynamic system that continuously redistributes the planet's water resources. According to data from NASA, this intricate cycle begins with the evaporation of water from Earth's surface, primarily from oceans, lakes, and rivers. As the sun's energy heats the Earth, water molecules rise into the atmosphere, forming clouds through a process known as condensation. These clouds eventually release their moisture through precipitation, which can fall as rain, snow, sleet, or hail. This precipitation nourishes the land, replenishes freshwater sources, and provides the essential resource for life on our planet. Additionally, some of this water infiltrates into the ground to become groundwater, while the rest flows into rivers and streams, eventually making its way back to the oceans. This continuous journey of water, driven by the sun's energy and Earth's gravitational forces, regulates our planet's climate, shapes landscapes, and supports ecosystems. NASA's data and research are crucial in understanding the intricacies of this global water cycle, helping us manage and conserve this precious resource in the face of changing environmental conditions.
